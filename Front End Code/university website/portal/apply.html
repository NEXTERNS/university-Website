    Take the first step on your journey to Capital University.
    Our admission processes are designed to be straightforward and transparent and our team is here to help along the way.

    .

    First Time in College applicants are applicants who have recently graduated from high school and have not taken college courses after graduation.

    Continuing Education applicants are applicants interested in Bachelor's Degree Completion, Accelerated Nursing (BSN), Capital City Teacher Residency, or Post Degree Education Licensure and Education Endorsements.

    Transfer applicants are applicants who have taken college courses after high school graduation and are looking to complete their degree at Capital in daytime courses.

    Graduate applicants are applicants who are interested in obtaining graduate degrees in business, counseling, education, music, or nursing. 

    Seminary applicants are applicants who are interested in taking any courses at Trinity Lutheran Seminary at Capital University.
    Capital University Law School applicants are applicants who are interested in taking any courses at Capital University Law School.

    Summer Institute in Science and Math applicants are applicants who are interested in taking select science and math courses over the summer.
    Non-Degree applicants are applicants who are interested in taking courses, but not seeking a degree or certificate.
